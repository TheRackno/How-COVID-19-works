COVID-19 has taken the world by storm and has continued to plague the earth to this day, many are infected, many are death and it continues to spread...

BUT HOW EXACTLY DOES IT WORK?

Let's start with what virus are

Virus are categoried by there structural make up

These are the 2 categories viruses are put in, they determine their shape, size and generic material. A virus main goal is to attack cells and replicate and depending on how a virus acts or what it attacks, will determine how dangerous the virus is.

One such virus is the Coronavirus

Despite what you may have heard Coronavirus is NOT the name of the 2019 virus but rather a group of virus or a genome group that are of similar type and are most of the time are quite harmless, mostly resulting in nothing more than the common cold.

The coronavirus has multiple variants and it's these variants is where the real problem starts...

Back in 2003 a variant of coronavirus broke out in china and spread to 4 other countries,
This variant was dubbed SARS

it's fatality rate was small but it showed that the coronavirus was capable of doing damage.

This leads to the most recent variant of coronavirus:

This virus was dubbed SARS-CoV2 also known as COVID-19 or the Coronavirus 19

This variant is sufficiently more dangerous and spreadable than it's previous variant and has resulted in worldwide infection and death

How COVID-19 works is that it latches its spiky surface proteins to receptors on healthy cells, especially those in your lungs.

It takes control of the cell and replicates itself spreading to the rest of the body and killing the cell

It's via infected person's droplet's of coughing, sneezing or breathing and enters the body giving the virus a passage to the mucous membranes in your throat.

Within 2 to 14 days, your immune system may respond with symptoms including:

  • Fever
  • A cough
  • Shortness of breath or trouble breathing
  • Fatigue
  • Chills, sometimes with shaking
  • Body aches
  • Headache
  • A sore throat
  • Congestion or a runny nose
  • Loss of taste
  • Loss of smell
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea
    •
The virus moves down your respiratory tract, COVID-19 is more likely to go deeper than viruses like the common cold. Your lungs become inflamed, making it tough for you to breathe, leading to pneumonia, an infection of the tiny air sacs called alveoli) inside your lungs where your blood exchanges oxygen and carbon dioxide. For some, the symptoms end with a cough and a fever, but for other the infection gets more severe
